What is anonymity?




Anonymity, especially online, is prone to becoming topic of debate- but what is it?
Anonymity is the concept of keeping people or persons anonymous, or the presence of
a means to providing complete privacy and total facelessness. The concept of anonymity
on the internet is problematic, as while it provides great benefits, it can also serve
as a means in which poor behavior, which generally would not be accepted in society,
becomes shameless and without consequence. To be able to speak one's mind without fear
of social reprocussions is powerful. No other saying is more relevant in this case
than that which goes "give a man a mask and he'll show his true face".



Online harrassment, sometimes referred to as "cyberbullying", is a common driver for
anti-anonymity thought. All too often, we see people, who are often vulnerable, subjected
to this cruel sort of harrassment. Remarks about appearence, controversy, race or ethnic
background, political beliefs, etc... are both nasty and common sights on the internet.
There is a belief that this generally occurs on social media and amongst youth, but in
reality, it's not uncommon to see fully grown adults attacking each other from behind
their screens. Aside from being incredibly childish and rude, real harm can come from this.
All too often we hear stories of people that commit self-harm, or in some extreme cases,
suicide, as a result of being victim to cyber-bullying.
